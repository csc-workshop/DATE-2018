Participate in the CSC-Workshop of DATE 2018:

The CSC-Workshop (W07) invites you to a threefold discussion to enable multidisciplinary excellence in EDA research.

Background

The term Electronic Design Automation (EDA) has been coined in the early 1980th with the invention of hardware description languages (such as VHDL and Verilog), the first hardware circuit simulators and logic synthesis. From this time, EDA has as a category of software tools for designing electronic systems such as integrated circuits and printed circuit boards. Since a modern semiconductor chip can have billions of components, EDA tools are essential for their design. At the beginning of the 21th century, the design and verification challenges forced a significant raise in the considered abstraction level concerns of EDA. Since that time, Electronic System Level (ESL) has gained major attention and the integrated specification and testing of hardware and software for large Systems-on-Chip (SoC) has been established. While the levels of abstraction have become larger, the considered physical properties yield, timing, power consumption, aging became wider and more difficult to handle. These overall aspects of EDA are further broadened through analogue circuit and MEMS design in combination with a 3D integration of these heterogeneous components.

Motivation

Microchips in the form of SoCs are already the cornerstone of our modern highly automated world. The heart of building microchips are reliable, highly efficient programs which allow to describe, validate and execute the individual steps of the design – from specifying the application to manufacturing, integrating and maintaining the product. Providing these tools is the Challenge of the EDA (Electronic Design Automation). Automating the integration of an ever-growing number of complex and mostly physically entangled functionalities from multiple application domains, traditional EDA research is facing many disruptive changes and the dependency between traditional EDA, EDA research and other system design and validation disciplines is expected to become even more important. Fully automated driving is a very good example for such a multidisciplinary subject where EDA support is required to obtain a solution that is safe, secure and economically feasible. Since the overall product will only be successful if large teams of experts with different background contribute to a joint solution, share their knowledge and collaborate. For this reason it becomes more and more important to foster multidisciplinary excellence in EDA research. With the CSC-Workshop we are asking for your opinion and active participation to test and challenge our hypothesis that the EDA research community requires new approaches to contribute, share and collaborate.

Based on invited talks we introduce you to problems and new solutions in the three main topic areas:

Contribute

Do we need new ways how we contribute to EDA research?

Can ad-hoc unconferences (which are born from the desire to share and learn in an open environment) help to enable new contributions in EDA research?
Share

How to evaluate and improve e.g. reproducibility of research results?

Can artifact evaluation bring a significant boost in sharing truly reproducible research results?
Collaborate

Do we collaborate well between EDA research, industry and other disciplines?

What are the requirements for a well-organized collaboration between the heterogeneous stakeholders of EDA? How to align the researchers, manufacturers, designers, software-developers and application engineers from different domains, such that we can identify new needs and realize faster exploitation of results? Which tools and methods are available to bridge the gaps?

Professionals and experts in each of these three areas pitch problems and new solutions for a joint discussion and a subsequent in-depth dialogue in a World Café session.

World Café

Providing time for an informal introduction of the participants, and encouraging to meet on a par with each other, the CSC-Workshop will enable you to actively contribute, compare and discuss your personal points of view. After lunch, each of the three workshop topics is allocated to a meeting-table and a topic-oriented table-host, organizing the discussion as a World Café. Within three phases (30 min each) the participants can join a table for an in-depth interactive dialogue with the other table-guests and the table-host. The table-hosts organize the discussions and ensure, that most important viewpoints and arguments of the discussion are visibly documented. Based on that, the participants of each phase can take up and complement the individual aspects of the previous discussion. In the end, the results of the World Café are presented and a common conclusion is drawn within a final wrap-up session.
